# Canon

## Completion Object

Build the reimagined Alexandria backend runtime whose implementation remains
legible to future coding agents and whose governing aim is recursive
deep-research cognition with custody, replay, and pharma CI claim lineage.

## Binding Canon

The active reimagining inputs in this repo are:

- the candidate reimagined charter at `ALEXANDRIA_CHARTER.md`
- the root creation plan at `PLAN_TO_CREATE_ALEXANDRIA.md`
- the authority ledger at
  `docs/design-docs/reimagined_alexandria_authority_ledger_2026_04_21.md`
- the prior ratified Charter v1.0 at `canon/specs/ALEXANDRIA_CHARTER.md`
- the imported NLSpec snapshot under `canon/specs/`
- this canon note
- repo-local design docs, plans, and golden rules when they do not contradict
  the reimagined authority surface or an explicitly retained binding spec

For reimagining work, old numbered NLSpecs are not automatically binding. Treat
them as legacy inputs until an audit classifies each clause as retained,
superseded, donor material, or requiring rewrite.

For implementation work, do not invent behavior from chat. If the authority
surface is unresolved, produce or update the authority note before coding.

## Imported-Spec Reading Rule

Read imported specs semantically and through the authority ledger.

For reimagining work, read `ALEXANDRIA_CHARTER.md`,
`PLAN_TO_CREATE_ALEXANDRIA.md`, and the authority ledger before legacy specs.

For implementation work that depends on a legacy NLSpec, the imported spec must
be read directly and its audit status must be checked. A conflicted or
unaudited legacy clause cannot silently govern implementation.

Repo-local design docs, plans, architecture summaries, review notes, and
external mirrors may help agents translate, sequence, or audit canon, but they
do not substitute for ratified authority or retained specs.

Binding:
- behavior
- proof obligations
- continuity expectations
- escalation rules
- retrieval, evidence, and synthesis requirements

Non-binding unless re-stated locally:
- historical file paths such as `scripts/run_loop.py`
- brownfield-preservation instructions that only made sense in an older repo
- historical module names and folder topology
- historical storage and deployment choices

## Non-Authority

These may be useful as reference, but they do not govern this repo:
- historical handoff chains
- historical outputs and snapshots
- old repo structure
- external trackers that are not mirrored in the repo
